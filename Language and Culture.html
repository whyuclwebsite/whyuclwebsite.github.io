Language and Culture: Get To Know The Course!

So you're interested in learning langugages and think that it might be a good idea to pursue them into further education? Then UCL's Language and Culture course is definitely worth checking out!

If you like the sound of a flexible degree programme that you can customise to suit your own interests and strengths at a well-renowned universtiy - then you're in the right place!


About Me

First and foremost, I am a first year undergraduate Language and Culture student at UCL. Amongst other things, I am an amateur beginner coder, an avid reader and a hopeful writer.
The most important thing I have come to realise is that there is no 'typical language student' - we're a mixed, eclectic bunch!

For me personally, the main advantages of the Language and Culture course is the ability to tailor your own degree. That's the great thing about becoming a language student right here at UCL, you are in control and your options only become greater over the four year course!
My timetable is currently made up of:

  • Use of French: French Grammar
  • French Oral Classes
  • Reading French Texts: Medieval French Literature
  • Spanish Classes: Grammar, Oral, Reading, Writing, Culture
  • Poetry of the Afterlife: English Literature
  • Language and Culture (Including things like gender studies, memory studies, the concept of culture and more!)

Your Language and Culture Degree

Like what you hear so far? Then maybe you'd be interested to know how the course could work for you. Why UCL? Why Language and Culture?

Let's start with a simple run down of the course for you:

This distinctive programme provides enormous flexibility for you to customise your degree according to your linguistic and cultural interests. You will combine the study of two languages, with the possibility of changing language after the first year. You will select a focus in a chosen area of cultural studies: cultural history; film studies; history of art; linguistics or literary studies.
